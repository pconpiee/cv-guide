Before the CV · A Different Question

Most people know how to write a bullet.
Few know which story their life is telling.

The career tools here help you articulate what you carry. But if you are not sure what you carry — or if the CV work feels hollow, like you are polishing something that does not yet feel like yours — there is somewhere else to begin. Four conversations. Twelve weeks. The question underneath the job question.

The Table → The Way →

How to use this toolkit

In order, or wherever you are in the process.

1
Know your story
Use the Career Folder method to collect your work — projects, impact, metrics — before you write a single bullet.
2
Write the CV
Use the CV Guide to structure, format, and sharpen every bullet until it proves something rather than describes something.
3
Build your stories
Use Interview Prep to map your best accomplishments to common questions — before the interview, not during it.
4
Practice out loud
Record yourself. Three times minimum. The first time you read it. The third time you own it.
